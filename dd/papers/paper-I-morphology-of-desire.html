The Morphology of Desire

Buddhist Śūnyatā, AI-Mediated Satiation, and a New Framework for Understanding Human Want

§ Abstract 要旨

Desire is typically framed as a lack that seeks its missing object. We propose the inverse: desire is the morphology of an interior hollow — a shape the self adopts around an absence. Drawing on Buddhist śūnyatā (emptiness) and contemporary work on AI-mediated satiation, we identify three canonical hollow-shapes — arrow, sphere, and star — each defining a distinct phenomenology of want. The framework reframes both consumer behavior and contemplative practice as operations on hollow-shape, not on the object of desire.

“Desire is not the absence of an object, but the morphology of an interior hollow — arrow, sphere, or star.” 欲望は対象の欠如ではなく、内部の空洞の形態 — 矢・球・星 — である。

§ Key findings 主要な発見
01

Three hollow-shapes exhaust canonical desire.

Arrow (directed wanting), sphere (homeostatic balance), and star (receptive joy-at-the-accidental) together cover desire's typology without remainder across the surveyed cases.

矢・球・星の三形状で古典的な欲望の類型は余りなく覆われる。

02

LLM-mediation collapses arrows into spheres.

Prolonged AI-mediated satiation converts directed wanting into a diffuse equilibrium state — a measurable shift on the shape-lattice from pointed to rounded.

LLM媒介は矢を球へと畳み込み、欲望を拡散した均衡状態に変える。

03

Contemplative practice operates on shape, not object.

Classical śūnyatā practice is re-read as hollow-shape transformation (star-ification) rather than object-suppression — yielding a testable predicate for practice efficacy.

空の修行は対象の抑圧でなく形の変換(星化)である。
