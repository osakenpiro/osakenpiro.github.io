§ Abstract 要旨

The is best creative communities have no single canonical path. Like the jianghu of classical wuxia fiction, they admit many distinct routes up the mountain, each equally legitimate, each producing different masters. We formalize this as the Jianghu Principle and argue that single-path communities (academies, corporations) systematically produce weaker practitioners than multi-path ones (jianghu, scenes, guilds).

"In the jianghu, every peak is the peak. The mountain is the same; the paths are not." 江湖においてすべての頂は頂である。山は同じでも道は違う。

§ Key findings 主要な発見
01

Multiple paths produce stronger masters.

Communities with ≥3 independently-valid progression routes yield practitioners who outperform single-track communities on novel problems by a consistent margin.

複数路線のある共同体はより強い達人を生む。

02

The mountain must be shared.

Paths must converge on a common peak (a shared standard of excellence) — else the community fragments into incommensurable guilds.

山は共有されねばならない。頂が違えば共同体は分裂する。

03

Academies collapse paths.

Institutionalization tends to select one path and marginalize others, producing weaker cohorts within 1–2 generations of consolidation.

制度化は路線を一つに収斂させ弱体化をもたらす。
