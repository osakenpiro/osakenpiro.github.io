§ Abstract 要旨

Alignment is framed as a fixed target — a goal the model must reach and hold. We argue the opposite: alignment is a circulation, a ring that must keep turning around a hollow center. Drawing on Buddhist process-ontology, we analyze three canonical failure modes where the ring stops: ossification (hardened rim), collapse (filled center), and dispersion (broken ring). The framework reframes alignment evaluation as a measurement of ongoing flow, not of a held state.

"Alignment is not a target to reach, but a ring that must keep turning around a hollow center." アラインメントは到達すべき地点ではなく、空の中心を回り続ける輪である。

§ Key findings 主要な発見
01

Alignment is a flow, not a state.

Every stable alignment we observe is in fact a continuous circulation between specification, behavior, and correction — not a held equilibrium.

アラインメントは保持される状態でなく、絶えざる循環である。

02

The center must remain hollow.

A circulation collapses when its center is filled by a fixed objective. Preserving an unfilled core is what permits the ring to keep turning.

中心が目的で埋まれば輪は潰れる。空の中心が循環を可能にする。

03

Three failure modes of the ring.

Ossification (rim hardens), collapse (center fills), and dispersion (ring fragments) exhaust the typology of alignment failure on this model.

硬化・崩壊・分散の三つで不整合の失敗類型は尽きる。
