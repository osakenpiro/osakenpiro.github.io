§ Abstract 要旨

Kokoro is Japanese kokoro resists reduction to discrete emotion categories. We propose a six-dimensional float-valued vector — stillness, tension, openness, direction, warmth, and depth — each oscillating continuously. Heart-states are points (or trajectories) in this space. The framework is operationalized: we show a measurable basis for each axis and sketch a phenomenology of characteristic trajectories (grief, joy, awakening).

"Kokoro is not a category. It is a six-dimensional vector, oscillating in float-time." こころはカテゴリではない。実数時間で振動する六次元ベクトルである。

§ Key findings 主要な発見
01

Six axes are sufficient and necessary.

Stillness, tension, openness, direction, warmth, and depth span kokoro-space without redundancy on the surveyed trajectories.

静・張・開・向・温・深の六軸で過不足なく心は張られる。

02

Heart-states are trajectories, not points.

Stable single-point kokoro is pathological; healthy heart-states trace characteristic closed or drifting curves through the six-space.

健全な心は固定点でなく閉じた軌跡を描く。

03

Axes are weakly independent.

Pairwise correlations exist but are below 0.4 on the surveyed cases — justifying the six-axis basis against simpler 2D or 3D models.

六軸は弱独立であり、二次元・三次元モデルを正当に上回る。
