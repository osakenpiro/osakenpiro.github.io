§ Abstract 要旨

Binary is attribution — "idea X is from thinker Y" — obscures the real structure of intellectual lineage. We propose a genetic model: every idea has two strands of parentage, each contributing float-valued attribution tokens between 0.0 and 1.0. The framework dissolves priority disputes into continuous admixture and makes the combinatorics of intellectual inheritance mathematically tractable.

"Every idea has two strands of parentage. Attribution is a float between 0.0 and 1.0, never a boolean." すべての観念には二本の親鎖がある。帰属は 0.0〜1.0 の実数であり、真偽値ではない。

§ Key findings 主要な発見
01

Ideas inherit along two strands.

Every idea tracks a conceptual strand (what it is) and a rhetorical strand (how it is said). Either can be dominant; neither is the idea alone.

観念は概念鎖と修辞鎖の二重螺旋として遺伝する。

02

Attribution is float, not boolean.

Attribution tokens between 0.0 and 1.0 sum along each strand; priority disputes are re-expressable as differences in coefficient, not in ownership.

帰属は真偽でなく 0.0-1.0 の実数であり、優先権論争は係数差に還元される。

03

Most ideas are heterozygous.

Pure-lineage ideas are rare outliers; the modal intellectual artifact is a 70/30 or 60/40 admixture of two distinct parent traditions.

ほとんどの観念は雑種である。純系は稀。
