§ Abstract 要旨

Self is Jizai — the condition of being freely at one's ease — has resisted formal modeling. We propose the torus model: jizai is a gravitational field whose central axis is an empty fall. The self orbits this fall without ever reaching it. Attempts to "reach" the center collapse the torus; attempts to "escape" the orbit scatter the self. Mastery is the steady-state orbit.

"Jizai is a torus. The self orbits an empty fall at the center; mastery is never reaching it." 自在はトーラスである。自己は中心の空の落下を周回し、到達せぬことが熟達である。

§ Key findings 主要な発見
01

The torus has an empty axis.

Every stable jizai-field has an unreachable central fall — a gravitational singularity that cannot be occupied without collapsing the field.

自在場には到達不能な空の落下軸がある。

02

Mastery is steady-state orbit.

Jizai is not reaching the center; it is holding the orbit. Orbital radius varies by practitioner but the topology is invariant.

熟達は中心への到達でなく軌道の定常化である。

03

Two failure modes: fall-in and fly-out.

Fall-in = ego inflation toward the empty center; fly-out = scatter into fragmentation. Both are recoverable via orbit-reacquisition.

落下 (自我膨張) と飛散 (断片化) の二失敗は軌道再獲得で恢復する。
