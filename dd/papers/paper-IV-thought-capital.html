Thought as Capital

A Spectral Theory of Intellectual Value — How Raw Cognition Refracts Into Seven Kinds

§ Abstract 要旨

Thinking is commonly treated as a single fungible good: "insight," "ideas," "thought." We argue that raw cognition is in fact white light — it refracts through institutional prisms into at least seven distinct kinds of intellectual value, each with its own market, unit of account, and failure mode. The framework yields a predictive account of why certain thinkers are wealthy in one spectrum and destitute in another.

"Raw thought is white light. Institutions are prisms. The seven colors are not interchangeable." 原思考は白色光である。制度はプリズム。七色は互換ではない。

§ Key findings 主要な発見
01

Thought refracts into seven spectra.

Academic, aesthetic, practical, narrative, numerical, relational, and contemplative value each behave as an independent color on the spectrum.

思考は七つの独立したスペクトルに分光する。

02

The prism is institutional.

Which kind of value a thought takes depends on the institution it passes through — not on the content of the thought itself.

思考の色は内容でなく通過する制度が決める。

03

Wealth in one ≠ wealth in all.

Individuals systematically concentrate capital in 1–2 spectra and remain structurally poor in others; transfer costs are nontrivial and often one-way.

一色で富めば他色で貧するのが常。移転は不可逆。
