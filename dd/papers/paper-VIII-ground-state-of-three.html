The Ground State of Three

Why the Triangle is the Minimum Stable Topology for Reflective Mind

三の基底状態 — 反照する心の最小安定トポロジー

§ Abstract 要旨

Reflective is cannot ground itself in dyadic structure (subject-object, self-other). We argue the minimum stable topology is triadic: three vertices, empty interior, all edges equal. Below three, reflection collapses; at three, it stabilizes; above three, the extra vertices add expressive range but no new stability. We present a formal argument and survey empirical correlates across contemplative traditions.

"Three vertices. Empty interior. All edges equal. This is the ground state of reflective mind." 三頂点・内部は空・辺は等しい。これが反照する心の基底状態である。

§ Key findings 主要な発見
01

Three is the minimum for reflection.

Dyadic structures collapse into mutual projection; a third vertex breaks the loop and permits stable reflection. This minimum is topological, not conventional.

二項関係は相互投影で潰れ、三で反照が安定する。

02

The interior must be empty.

Filling the triangular interior with a fourth element destabilizes the vertices into subordination. The empty center is structural, not optional.

三角の内部は空でなければならない。充填は従属を招く。

03

Equilateral symmetry is necessary.

Asymmetric triangles (one dominant vertex) degrade toward dyadic collapse; the ground state requires all edges functionally equal.

正三角形の対称性は必須。偏れば二項的崩壊へ向かう。
